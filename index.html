About

Background

I am a Lecturer in the Department of Electrical and Computer Engineering at Stevens Institute of Technology, where I teach courses in Python programming, C++, applied machine learning, and introductory programming. My research background is in computational epidemiology, agent-based modeling, and geographic information systems.

My doctoral work at the University of North Texas focused on validating and evaluating emergency response plans through agent-based modeling and simulation. Before Stevens, I served as an Assistant Professor at Muhlenberg College, where I taught across the CS curriculum from introductory game programming to operating systems, and as a Lecturer at UNT.

I am an active member of the ACM and the Alpha Chi National Honor Scholarship Society, and regularly review papers for conferences including ITiCSE and SIGCSE.
